Whatâ€™s a UID and GID? 
	UID: Unique Identifier for Users of the application that owns the process
	GID: Unique Identifier for Group of Users of the the application that owns the process
	PID: Unique Process Identifier for specific process
	PPID: The PID of the parent process

What are the differences between the UID and GID of system owned processes and user applications?
	Each app has a unique UID and GID. 
	They have different permissions.
	Limited permissions are given to installed apps within an assigned directory.
	GID 0 is root.
	GID in 10000 series are installed applications.
